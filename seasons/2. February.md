**February (2月)**

February is usually the coldest month in Japan, but it is also one of the most rewarding months for winter travel. Snow conditions are excellent in Hokkaido and the mountains, while many regions outside the snow belt remain dry and sunny.

Some highlights of traveling in Japan in February include:

* Snow festivals take place in several parts of the country, and one of the best known is [Hokkaido Winter Festivals](../events/festivals/Hokkaido%20Winter%20Festivals.md).

* February is one of the best months for skiing and snowboarding, especially in Hokkaido and Nagano, where powder snow is often at its peak.

* Outdoor hot springs are especially enjoyable in February, when the contrast between the cold air and hot mineral water feels most dramatic.

* In warmer parts of Japan, the first plum blossoms begin to appear toward the end of the month, offering an early sign of spring before cherry blossom season begins.
