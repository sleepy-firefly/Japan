**September (9月)**

September is another transition month in Japan. Early in the month it can still feel very summery, but by the end of September the weather is often more comfortable and the first signs of autumn begin to appear.

Some highlights of traveling in Japan in September include:

* Crowds are often lighter after the summer holiday period, so it can be a more relaxed time for city trips and cultural sightseeing.

* In Hokkaido and high mountain areas, early autumn colors can begin surprisingly soon, making northern Japan especially appealing toward the end of the month.

* Rice fields start turning golden in rural areas, so countryside scenery becomes particularly beautiful at this time of year.

* September is also part of typhoon season, so it is a good idea to keep plans flexible and check the weather forecast regularly.

* For gaming and tech-entertainment travel, September often features [Tokyo Game Show](../events/otaku/Tokyo%20Game%20Show.md).
