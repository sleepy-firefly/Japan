**March (3月)**

March is a transition month between winter and spring. Temperatures become milder in much of the country, and the first signs of blossom season begin to appear, especially in the south and in warmer cities.

Some highlights of traveling in Japan in March include:

* Early cherry blossoms begin to open in the warmest parts of Japan toward the end of the month, while plum blossoms can still be seen in some areas.

* March is a pleasant time for city travel, since the weather is generally more comfortable than in deep winter and the heavy summer humidity has not yet arrived.

* Ski season is still possible in Hokkaido and in higher mountain resorts, so March can be a good month if you want to combine winter activities with milder weather elsewhere.

* Parks, castle grounds, and riversides become more lively as spring approaches, making it a good month for relaxed walking and sightseeing.

* For anime-focused travel in Tokyo, March often includes [AnimeJapan](../events/otaku/AnimeJapan.md), one of the country's biggest annual anime conventions.
