**January (1月)**

January is one of the coldest months in Japan, but it is also one of the best times to experience winter scenery, hot springs, and clear skies. In many parts of the country the air is dry and sunny, while Hokkaido and the Japanese Alps offer deep snow and excellent winter sports.

Some highlights of traveling in Japan in January include:

* New Year is one of the most important holidays in Japan, so temples and shrines are busy with people making their first visit of the year (see [Temple Index](../temples/Temple%20Index.md)). It is a special cultural experience, but many shops and attractions close for a few days at the beginning of the month.

* January is a great time to enjoy onsen, especially outdoor baths surrounded by snow in northern Japan and mountain resort towns.

* Winter illuminations are still running in many cities in early January, so evenings can be especially atmospheric.

* In snowy regions, the landscapes are beautiful and peaceful, and winter wildlife trips, snowy villages, and mountain scenery are at their best.
