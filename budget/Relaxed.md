**Relaxed Budget Plan (Per Person, 3 Weeks, First Trip to Japan, Not Luxury)**

This is a relaxed-comfort per-person plan for a 21-day Japan trip.
Assumption: both travelers pay separately with no shared costs, and spending style is similar.
Focus: traditions/history, temples, nature, festivals, daily life, and otaku culture.

Conversion used for consistency in this file: \$1 = ¥150.


**1) Core assumptions**


- Trip length: 21 days (20 nights)
- Traveler basis: 1 adult (per-person view)
- Style: relaxed comfort, smooth logistics, no luxury hotels
- Lodging mix: one well-located room in quality business or upper mid-range hotels
- Transport style: local trains/subways + intercity trains, with occasional taxi convenience
- Food style: regular restaurant dining, regional specialties, cafes, and occasional premium meal
- Shopping: moderate-to-high (including planned otaku spending)
- Flight cost: not included in the base totals (shown separately for one traveler from Gdansk and one from Minsk)

**5) Best value recommendation by goal**



&emsp;**Best overall relaxed-value choice: November**


- Great weather and scenery for comfortable full-day exploration
- Excellent for temples, gardens, history walks, and nature
- Tradeoff: higher prices in peak foliage areas


&emsp;**Best festival-heavy choice: July**


- Best overall access to major summer festivals
- Tradeoff: crowding and premium hotel pricing around event dates


&emsp;**Best lower-cost relaxed choice: June**


- Better room value for this comfort level
- Tradeoff: rain planning required

**7) Relaxed-budget rules that keep spending efficient**


- Book rooms 10-14 weeks early in peak months
- Use refundable rates where possible for flexibility
- Cluster expensive activities on selected days; keep simple neighborhood days between them
- Pre-book key museums/events to avoid costly last-minute alternatives
- Keep otaku shopping as a planned category, not incidental spend
- Synchronize arrivals to reduce idle hotel nights and transfer costs
