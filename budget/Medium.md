**Medium Budget Plan (Per Person, 3 Weeks, First Trip to Japan)**

This is a balanced per-person plan for a 21-day Japan trip.
Assumption: both travelers pay separately with no shared costs, and spending style is similar.
Focus: traditions/history, temples, nature, festivals, daily life, and otaku culture.

Conversion used for consistency in this file: \$1 = ¥150.

&emsp;&emsp;**1) Core assumptions**

- Trip length: 21 days (20 nights)
- Style: comfortable and flexible, but still cost-aware
- Lodging mix: one mid-range room (business hotels, occasional boutique stay)
- Transport style: mostly local trains/subways + 2-3 intercity moves
- Food style: mix of local restaurants, set lunches, casual izakaya, occasional splurge meal
- Shopping: moderate (souvenirs + planned otaku shopping)
- Flight cost: not included in the base totals (shown separately for one traveler from Gdansk and one from Minsk)

&emsp;&emsp;**5) Best value recommendation by goal**

&emsp;&emsp;**Best overall medium-value choice: June**

- Good prices relative to comfort level
- Great for temples, history, and slower local-life travel
- Tradeoff: more rainy days

&emsp;&emsp;**Best festival-heavy choice: July**

- Best month for iconic festivals and summer energy
- Tradeoff: higher prices and more crowd pressure

&emsp;&emsp;**Best comfort/weather balance: November**

- Excellent weather for city walks and nature
- Tradeoff: autumn hotspots can be expensive if booked late

&emsp;&emsp;**7) Medium-budget rules that protect value**

- Book rooms 8-12 weeks early (especially July/August/November)
- Keep a mixed day structure: one paid-heavy day, one low-cost day
- Reserve major timed entries in advance to avoid expensive same-day alternatives
- Use regional day passes where they clearly beat single-ticket costs
- Set a planned otaku shopping envelope before arrival
- Align arrivals with at least a half-day buffer
