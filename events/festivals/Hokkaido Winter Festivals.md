**Hokkaido Winter Festivals**

Hokkaido has three notable early-February winter experiences that travelers often combine into one trip: the Asahikawa Winter Festival, the Sapporo Snow Festival, and the Otaru Snow Light Path Festival. They are separate events in different cities, but all feature snow art, illuminated evening scenes, and cold-weather travel logistics.

&emsp;&emsp;**Asahikawa Winter Festival**

The Asahikawa Winter Festival is one of Hokkaido's best winter events and is held in early February. It is famous for its giant snow stage, large snow sculptures, and dramatic night illuminations.

During the day, visitors can enjoy detailed snow art, local food stalls, and winter activities for families. At night, the sculptures are lit up, creating a completely different atmosphere.

The festival is often combined with other winter trips in Hokkaido, such as hot springs, ski areas, and seasonal food experiences. Because February is peak winter season, warm clothing and snow-ready shoes are essential.

&emsp;&emsp;**Sapporo Snow Festival**

The Sapporo Snow Festival is one of Japan's most famous winter events, held in early February around Odori Park and nearby city venues. It is known for giant snow sculptures, evening illuminations, and a lively winter atmosphere.

Visitors usually spend time between large-scale snow art zones, food stalls, and night light-up areas. The event combines local culture with international snow sculpture teams, so each year has a different visual style.

&emsp;&emsp;**Otaru Snow Light Path Festival**

The Otaru Snow Light Path Festival is a winter illumination event usually held in February in Otaru. Candle-lit snow lanterns and soft light installations transform canal and side-street areas into one of Hokkaido's most atmospheric winter scenes.

Compared with large urban snow festivals, this event feels more intimate and walkable. It is especially popular in the evening when lights reflect on snow and canal water.

Because it is a smaller, more atmospheric event, Otaru is best treated as a separate walking experience rather than as a photo-heavy add-on to the bigger snow festivals.

&emsp;&emsp;**Practical info**

- Typical period for all three Hokkaido winter events: early February.
- Best strategy: visit daytime for sculpture detail and return at night for light-up scenes.
- Winter travel tip: book accommodation early and allow extra time for snowy transit.
